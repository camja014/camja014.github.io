# The Homelab - Part 1

Awhile back I found an entire subreddit entirely dedicated to building and showing off crazy home networking and server setups. We're talking full server racks complete with enterprise grade switches and routers. Inspired by some of the setups in this sub-reddit I decided I was going to setup a little homelab of my own. I already had most of the systems. But there is something about putting it all in big stack and doing a little bit of cable management that makes it look and feel so much more "hacker-y".

But the real reason behind doing this is to teach myself. I've been working in tech support for over 5 years now, and I've setup and managed plenty of my own systems over the years. But the nice thing about managing personal services and servers is that if you break something, it's not that big of a deal. I don't mind if my media server is down for a day or two. But if that was a production database server it would be another story. And that is something I don't have a lot of experience doing. After graduating in December, and starting the job hunt shortly afterwards, the question of how would I manage a cluster of resources in a way that keeps them secure, stable, and reliable was one I began thinking a lot more about.
